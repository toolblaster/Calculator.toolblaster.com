Still Got Questions? (FAQ)

What is a Systematic Investment Plan (SIP)?

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is an investment strategy where you invest a fixed amount of money at regular intervals, such as monthly. It's ideal for salaried individuals as it promotes disciplined saving and benefits from rupee cost averaging, which reduces the risk of market timing.

What is a Lumpsum investment?

A Lumpsum investment is a one-time, significant investment of a large amount of money. This strategy is suitable for individuals who have received a large amount of cash (like a bonus or inheritance) and want to give their money the maximum possible time to compound.

Which is better, SIP or Lumpsum?

Neither is definitively 'better'‚Äîthe right choice depends on your financial situation. SIP is generally better for regular earners who want to build wealth over time without worrying about market timing. Lumpsum is better for those who have a large sum of money available and a long time horizon, as it maximizes the power of compounding. Often, a combination of both strategies is the most effective approach.

Which gives higher returns: SIP or Lumpsum?

Statistically, if the market goes up over the long term, a Lumpsum investment will generally yield higher absolute returns because the entire amount is invested from day one, benefiting fully from compounding. However, a SIP can provide better-annualized returns (IRR) if the market is volatile, as it averages out the purchase cost. Lumpsum offers a higher potential reward but also carries a higher risk of bad timing.

Can I switch from a SIP to a Lumpsum investment?

You don't really "switch" between them. A SIP is a method of investing regularly. You can stop your SIP at any time and the money you've invested will remain in the fund. If you later receive a large sum of money, you can make a separate Lumpsum investment into the same fund or a different one.

What if I have a small lumpsum amount? Should I invest it at once?

If you have a smaller lumpsum amount (e.g., ‚Çπ25,000 or ‚Çπ50,000) and a long investment horizon (5+ years), it's generally fine to invest it all at once. The risk of timing the market is less significant with smaller amounts. Alternatively, you could use a Systematic Transfer Plan (STP), where you place the lumpsum in a low-risk liquid fund and transfer smaller amounts into an equity fund over a few months.
