Maximizing Your Deductions: Key Tax-Saving Strategies

If the calculator shows the Old Regime is beneficial, you can further reduce your tax outgo by making smart use of these popular deductions.

  • Fully Utilize Section 80C (up to ₹1.5 Lakh):

    This is the foundation of tax saving. It includes your EPF contributions, life insurance premiums, PPF, ELSS, and home loan principal repayment.

  • Leverage Your Home Loan (up to ₹2 Lakh):

    The interest you pay on your home loan is deductible under Section 24, making it one of the most significant tax-saving tools for homeowners.

  • Go Beyond with NPS (up to ₹50,000):

    Section 80CCD(1B) offers an additional deduction for contributions to the National Pension System (NPS), over and above the 80C limit.

  • Secure Your Health (Section 80D):

    Deductions for health insurance premiums for yourself, your family, and your parents can provide significant tax relief while ensuring financial security.

Understanding the Old vs. New Tax Regimes

Old Regime

This traditional regime allows you to claim numerous deductions and exemptions (like 80C, HRA, LTA, etc.) to reduce your taxable income, but it comes with higher tax rates at certain income levels.

New Regime

This is a simplified regime with lower, more streamlined tax slabs. However, you must forgo most of the common tax deductions and exemptions available under the Old Regime.

Which is Better?

There's no single answer. The best choice depends entirely on your salary structure and how many deductions you can claim. Our salary tax calculator is the best way to compare your specific situation.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Can I switch between the old and new regimes every year?

Yes, if you are a salaried individual without any business income, you have the flexibility to choose between the old and new tax regimes each financial year when you file your income tax return.

What is the Standard Deduction?

The Standard Deduction is a flat deduction of ₹50,000 that salaried individuals and pensioners can claim from their gross salary, reducing their taxable income. As of the latest budget, this deduction is available under both the Old and New Tax Regimes.

How does the Section 87A rebate work?

Section 87A provides a tax rebate to individuals with a net taxable income below a certain threshold. In the New Regime, if your taxable income is up to ₹7 lakh, the rebate makes your tax liability zero. In the Old Regime, the threshold is ₹5 lakh. This is why you might see 'No Tax' even if your income falls into a 5% slab.

Why is my tax not zero even though my income is below the rebate limit?

This usually happens because the rebate is calculated on your 'Net Taxable Income', not your 'Gross Salary'. For example, if your gross salary is ₹7.2 lakh and you have no deductions, your taxable income is above the ₹7 lakh threshold in the New Regime, so you have to pay tax. The calculator automatically applies the rebate where applicable.
