Understanding Your Investment Options

Our calculator adapts to your needs. Here's a quick guide to each mode:

SIP (Systematic Investment Plan)

Invest fixed amounts regularly into mutual funds. Purpose: Build wealth consistently over time.

Ideal Use: Long-term goals (retirement, education). Key Benefit: Discipline, Rupee Cost Averaging. Risk/Return: Medium-High/Variable.
Lumpsum

Invest a large, one-time amount. Purpose: Maximize compounding time for large sums.

Ideal Use: Bonus, inheritance, long horizon. Key Benefit: Maximizes time for growth. Risk/Return: High/Variable (Timing sensitive).
RD (Recurring Deposit)

Save fixed amounts monthly/quarterly with guaranteed returns. Purpose: Achieve short-term goals safely.

Ideal Use: Short-term goals (1-3 yrs), emergency fund buildup. Key Benefit: Guaranteed return, capital safety. Risk/Return: Risk-Free/Fixed.
FD (Fixed Deposit)

Invest a lumpsum for a fixed period with guaranteed returns. Purpose: Park funds safely, earn predictable interest.

Ideal Use: Capital protection, very short term goals. Key Benefit: Absolute safety, predictable income. Risk/Return: Risk-Free/Fixed.
SWP (Systematic Withdrawal Plan)

Withdraw fixed amounts regularly from your investment corpus. Purpose: Generate tax-efficient retirement income.

Ideal Use: Post-retirement income generation. Key Benefit: Tax-efficient, regular cash flow. Risk/Return: Low-Medium/Variable (Stable).
Goal Planner

Calculate the monthly investment needed to reach a specific target. Purpose: Turn dreams into actionable plans.

Ideal Use: Planning any financial goal (car, house, retirement). Key Benefit: Quantifies required SIP/investment. Risk/Return: N/A (Planning Tool).

Behind the Numbers: The Formulas We Use

SIP / RD Future Value
M = P * [((1 + i)^n - 1) / i] * (1 + i)
  • M: Maturity Amount
  • P: Periodic Investment
  • i: Periodic Rate of Interest
  • n: Number of Periods
Lumpsum / FD Future Value
A = P * (1 + r/k)^(k*t)
  • A: Future Value
  • P: Principal Amount
  • r: Annual Interest Rate
  • k: Compounding Frequency
  • t: Number of Years

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Why is an inflation-adjusted SIP calculator important?

An inflation-adjusted SIP calculator is crucial because it gives you a realistic picture of your future wealth. It shows you the 'real value' of your money, helping you set a more accurate target corpus that accounts for the rising cost of living.

Which is better for short-term goals: RD or FD?

Both are excellent for short-term goals. An RD (Recurring Deposit) is ideal for saving a fixed amount every month. A FD (Fixed Deposit) is perfect if you have a one-time lumpsum to invest. Both offer guaranteed returns and capital safety.

When should I use a SWP (Systematic Withdrawal Plan)?

An SWP is designed for the distribution phase of your life, typically post-retirement. You use it to draw a regular, tax-efficient income from your accumulated corpus. Our SWP calculator helps you plan how much you can withdraw and how long your money will last.

What is the difference between a SIP and a Lumpsum investment?

A SIP involves investing small amounts regularly, which is great for discipline and averaging out market risk. A Lumpsum investment is a one-time large investment, which gives your entire capital the maximum time to compound but carries higher timing risk.
