Behind the Numbers: The Formulas We Use

SIP / RD Future Value
M = P * [((1 + i)^n - 1) / i] * (1 + i)
  • M: Maturity Amount
  • P: Periodic Investment
  • i: Periodic Rate of Interest
  • n: Number of Periods
Lumpsum / FD Future Value
A = P * (1 + r/k)^(k*t)
  • A: Future Value
  • P: Principal Amount
  • r: Annual Interest Rate
  • k: Compounding Frequency
  • t: Number of Years

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Why is an inflation-adjusted SIP calculator important?

An inflation-adjusted SIP calculator is crucial because it gives you a realistic picture of your future wealth. It shows you the 'real value' of your money, helping you set a more accurate target corpus that accounts for the rising cost of living.

Which is better for short-term goals: RD or FD?

Both are excellent for short-term goals. An RD (Recurring Deposit) is ideal for saving a fixed amount every month. A FD (Fixed Deposit) is perfect if you have a one-time lumpsum to invest. Both offer guaranteed returns and capital safety.

When should I use a SWP (Systematic Withdrawal Plan)?

An SWP is designed for the distribution phase of your life, typically post-retirement. You use it to draw a regular, tax-efficient income from your accumulated corpus. Our SWP calculator helps you plan how much you can withdraw and how long your money will last.

What is the difference between a SIP and a Lumpsum investment?

A SIP involves investing small amounts regularly, which is great for discipline and averaging out market risk. A Lumpsum investment is a one-time large investment, which gives your entire capital the maximum time to compound but carries higher timing risk.
