Key Concepts for Smart Investing

The Magic of Compounding

Compounding is when you earn returns on your returns. Our calculator shows how this magical effect accelerates your wealth growth over time.

Rupee Cost Averaging

With SIPs, you automatically buy more units when the market is low and fewer when it's high. This averages out your cost and reduces timing risk.

The Impact of Inflation

Inflation reduces the purchasing power of your money. A corpus of ₹1 Crore in 20 years will not buy what ₹1 Crore buys today.

Behind the Numbers: The Formulas We Use

Our calculators use standard financial formulas to provide accurate projections. Here’s a look at the math behind the magic.

SIP / RD Future Value

This formula calculates the future value of a series of regular investments.

M = P * [((1 + i)^n - 1) / i] * (1 + i)
  • M: Maturity Amount
  • P: Periodic Investment
  • i: Periodic Rate of Interest
  • n: Number of Periods

Lumpsum / FD Future Value

The standard compound interest formula for a one-time investment.

A = P * (1 + r/k)^(k*t)
  • A: Future Value
  • P: Principal Amount
  • r: Annual Interest Rate
  • k: Compounding Frequency
  • t: Number of Years

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is an SIP calculator with inflation adjustment?

An SIP calculator with inflation adjustment is a financial tool that not only projects the future value of your Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) but also shows you what that future value will be worth in today's terms. It accounts for the gradual decrease in the purchasing power of money over time, giving you a more realistic financial forecast.

Why is my inflation-adjusted return lower than the total value?

Your total value shows the absolute amount of money you'll have, while the inflation-adjusted return (or "real value") shows what that money can actually buy. Because prices of goods and services tend to rise over time due to inflation, the ₹10 Lakhs you have in the future will buy less than ₹10 Lakhs today. Our calculator shows you this important difference.

How does this tool help in better financial planning?

By using a reliable financial calculator, you can set more realistic financial goals. If you need a corpus that has the purchasing power of ₹1 Crore today for your retirement in 20 years, this tool will help you calculate the actual (higher) target amount you need to achieve, ensuring you are not under-prepared.

What is a good monthly SIP amount to start with?

There is no one-size-fits-all answer. A good starting point is to invest at least 10-15% of your monthly income. The most important thing is to start early and stay consistent, even with a small amount like ₹500 or ₹1000 per month.
