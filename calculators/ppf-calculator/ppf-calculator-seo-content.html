Understanding the Public Provident Fund (PPF)

The PPF is a government-backed, long-term savings scheme that offers a powerful combination of safety, tax benefits, and attractive returns. It's a cornerstone of financial planning for millions of Indians.

  • Triple Tax Exemption (EEE Status):

    Your investment is tax-deductible under Section 80C, the interest earned is tax-free, and the final maturity amount is also tax-free. This is the highest level of tax efficiency available.

  • Guaranteed, Attractive Returns:

    The interest rate is set by the government and is typically higher than FD rates, offering a safe, reliable way to grow your money.

  • Long-Term Discipline:

    The 15-year lock-in period instills a disciplined approach to saving, making it an ideal tool for long-term goals like retirement or a child's education.

Understanding PPF Loans and Withdrawals

While PPF is a long-term scheme, it offers liquidity options through loans and partial withdrawals. Our calculator helps you instantly see what you're eligible for in any given year.

  • Loan Facility (Year 3 to 6):

    You can take a loan against your PPF balance from the third to the sixth financial year. The maximum loan amount is 25% of the balance at the end of the second year preceding the loan year.

  • Partial Withdrawal (From Year 7):

    From the seventh financial year onwards, you can make one partial withdrawal each year. The maximum amount is the lower of: 50% of the balance at the end of the previous year, or 50% of the balance at the end of the fourth year preceding the withdrawal year.

Behind the Numbers: The PPF Calculation Method

Unlike other investments, PPF interest is calculated monthly but compounded annually. Our calculator uses the official method for maximum accuracy.

  1. Monthly Interest Calculation: Interest for each month is calculated on the lowest balance in the account between the close of the 5th day and the end of the month.
  2. Annual Compounding: At the end of the financial year (March 31st), the total interest calculated for all 12 months is added to the PPF account balance.
Closing Balance = Opening + Investment + Interest

This process repeats for each year. Our calculator simulates this to project your final maturity value.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Can I withdraw money from my PPF account before 15 years?

Partial withdrawals are allowed from the 7th financial year onwards, subject to certain limits. Premature closure is only permitted under specific conditions like critical illness or for higher education purposes, and only after the account has completed 5 financial years.

What happens after the 15-year maturity period?

You have three options: 1) Withdraw the entire amount and close the account. 2) Extend the account in blocks of 5 years without making any further contributions (your balance will continue to earn interest). 3) Extend the account in blocks of 5 years with fresh contributions, continuing the investment as before.

Is the PPF interest rate fixed?

No, the interest rate for PPF is not fixed for the entire tenure. The Government of India reviews and announces the interest rate every quarter of the financial year. However, once an interest rate is set for a quarter, it applies to your entire balance for that period.
